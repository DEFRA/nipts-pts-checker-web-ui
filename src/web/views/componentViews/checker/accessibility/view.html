Accessibility statement for Check a pet travelling from GB to NI

This accessibility statement applies to the ‘Check a pet travelling from GB to NI’ service.

This website is run by the Department of Environment, Food and Rural Affairs.

We want as many people as possible to be able to use this website. For example, that means you should be able to:

  • use browser settings or plugins to change colours, contrast levels and fonts
  • use browser settings or other software to zoom in up to 300% without the text spilling off the screen
  • navigate most of the website using just a keyboard
  • navigate most of the website using speech recognition software

We've also made the website text as simple as possible to understand.

AbilityNet (opens in new tab) has advice on making your device easier to use if you have a disability.

How accessible this website is

We know some parts of this website are not fully accessible.

There are access issues for users who require audio feedback to navigate and interact with page content:

  • some page elements either do not contain discernible text or programmatically determined labels, or they are not descriptive enough for users to determine their function or purpose
  • non-text content does not have appropriate textual alternatives for these users
  • content structure is not properly conveyed, particularly relating to heading and grouping structure
  • some content is hidden from these users despite being available visibly
  • interactive elements are not marked up properly
  • it could be difficult for keyboard-only users to track the order in which some page elements receive focus because focus did not follow the expected order or was obscured by other content
  • interactive elements could be difficult to read when receiving keyboard focus due to their change in colour
  • some components are not fully compatible with voice activation software or were not labelled accessibly, meaning alternative voice commands are needed

Some colour combinations are low contrast and are likely to be difficult for people with low vision to read.

Interactive elements do not have sufficient labels or instructions.

Some error messages are not sufficiently clear, meaning it may be difficult for some users to complete form fields.

Reporting accessibility problems with this website

If you find any problems not listed on this page or think we're not meeting accessibility requirements, contact us by:

  • Email: NIPetTravel@apha.gov.uk
  • Phone: 03000 200 301 – from 8.30am to 5pm, Monday to Friday (excluding bank holidays)

We'll consider your request and get back to you within 5 days.

Enforcement procedure

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (the 'accessibility regulations'). If you're not happy with how we respond to your complaint, contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS) (opens in new tab).

If you live in Northern Ireland, complain to the Equalities Commission for Northern Ireland (ECNI) (opens in new tab).

Technical information about this website's accessibility

The Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs is committed to making its website accessible, in accordance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (opens in a new tab).

Compliance status

This website is partially compliant with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.1 (opens in new tab) AA standard, due to the non-compliances listed below.

Non-accessible content

The content listed below is non-accessible for the following reasons.

Non-compliance with the accessibility regulations

  • Interactive elements did not have sufficient visible labels or instructions to assist users in completing forms. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 3.3.2 Labels or Instructions (Level A) and 4.1.2 Name, Role, Value (Level A). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • The header has footer descendants, which is not valid and may result in parsing errors and some content being inaccessible to users. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 1.3.1 Info and Relationships (Level A). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Text did not contrast sufficiently to be discernible to low vision users. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum) (Level AA) and 1.4.6 Contrast (Enhanced) (Level AAA). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Error messages did not provide sufficient suggestions for users to resolve input errors. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 3.3.3 Error Suggestion (Level AA). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Interactive elements were obscured by other content when receiving keyboard focus, hindering keyboard navigation. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 2.4.11 Focus Not Obscured (Minimum) (Level AA) and 2.4.12 Focus Not Obscured (Enhanced) (Level AAA). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Non-text content did not have sufficient or appropriate textual alternatives for screen reader users. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 1.1.1 Non-text Content (Level A). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Heading structure was not properly conveyed to screen reader users. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 1.3.1 Info and Relationships (Level A). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Links whose purpose was conveyed through images had no accessible names, meaning they may not be accessible to users of screen reading and voice activation software. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 1.1.1 Non-text Content (Level A), 2.4.4 Link Purpose (In Context) (Level A), 2.5.3 Label in Name (Level A), 4.1.2 Name, Role, Value (Level A) and 2.4.9 Link Purpose (Link Only) (Level AAA). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Interactive elements were contained within other interactive elements in the markup, posing barriers to assistive technology users. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 1.3.1 Info and Relationships (Level A), 2.4.3 Focus Order (Level A) and 4.1.2 Name, Role, Value (Level A). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Form information was programmatically hidden from assistive technology users, which could make it difficult to progress through the service. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 1.3.1 Info and Relationships (Level A). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Form grouping was not programmatically conveyed, meaning it could be difficult for screen reader users to understand the purpose of some fields. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 1.3.1 Info and Relationships (Level A). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.
  • Headings and labels were not sufficiently descriptive of topic or purpose for screen readers. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criteria 2.4.6 Headings and Labels (Level AA). We are investigating how to fix this, and will review this again by September 2025.

Usability

Although the following do not fail to meet WCAG 2.2, they may prove challenging for users of the service.

  • Form grouping is marked up in a way that could be difficult for screen reader users to understand.
  • Interactive controls do not have clearly visible mouse cursor hover indicators.
  • Background gradients are present that could make it more difficult for some users to read page content.
  • Elements are not styled consistently throughout the service.
  • Error summary lists errors in a different order to the form fields.
  • The 'Back' links do not function with JavaScript-based assistive technologies.

We plan to review these by September 2025.

What we're doing to improve accessibility

We will monitor data to ensure employees are able to use the website and to identify any issues.

We plan to fix these by September 2025.
